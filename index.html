STOICISM

Eudaimonia

Eudaimonia is a central concept in Stoicism, an ancient philosophy that seeks wisdom and virtue as paths to happiness. In Stoicism, eudaimonia is considered the supreme goal of human life, the state of fullness and fulfillment that everyone should strive to achieve.

The only reason to study philosophy is to become a better person. Everything else, as Nietzsche said, is nothing more than a "critique of words with words." No school of thought believed more in this—the power of action over the power of ideas—than Stoicism, an ancient philosophical branch dating back to 3rd century B.C. Greece. It was Seneca, a Stoic philosopher from the Roman era, separate from the Academy, who stated quite directly that there was no other purpose in reading and studying than to lead a happy life.
